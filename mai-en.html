Conversational design

Mai, from AprendiZAP Encceja

Expanding access to education for iFood partners through a simple, human, and accessible conversational experience.

UX Writing Chatbot Design Education Social impact
Client

AprendiZAP

Study platform focused on expanding access to education for iFood partners.

Project year

2024

Project designed for the conversational assistant pre-launch context.

Goal

Drive enrollment

Guide users through a simple, welcoming, and clear path to the waiting list.

Context

Project context.

Mai connects iFood couriers and partners to the high-school completion program, guiding them toward Encceja through a personalized and supportive journey.

Challenge

Many partner workers did not complete high school and face time, access, and information barriers when trying to return to their studies.

Solution

A conversational assistant that guides program enrollment, identifies profiles, and adapts the journey to each person’s context.

Personas

Personas.

Three profiles informed the language, flow, and support decisions throughout the experience.

Marcos

28 years old · iFood delivery partner

Context

Has worked as a courier for 2 years and did not finish high school.

Pain points

  • Not enough time to search for information.
  • Unaware of free opportunities.
  • Feels that “it is too late” to go back to studying.

Carla

35 years old · Restaurant owner

Context

Runs a small restaurant listed on iFood and has not completed high school.

Pain points

  • Busy daily routine managing the business.
  • Uncertainty about the enrollment process.
  • Questions about certificate validity.

Rafael

32 years old · Curious user

Context

Reached the bot without a direct iFood connection, but is looking for study opportunities.

Pain points

  • Does not know whether they qualify for the benefits.
  • Needs guidance on what to do next.
  • Has doubts about eligibility.

Methodology

Project stages.

A three-track process to understand the context, organize the experience, and write the conversation clearly.

Research

Understanding the barriers, motivations, and real context of people entering the flow.

  • Audience and usage context analysis.
  • Journey and needs mapping.
  • Requirement gathering and validation.

Structure

Organizing flows, decision points, and the main path toward enrollment.

  • Journey definition by profile.
  • State and transition mapping.
  • Clarity prioritized at every step.

Conversation

Writing direct, welcoming messages consistent with the project tone.

  • Greeting, onboarding, and confirmations.
  • Error and recovery messages.
  • Fine-tuning tone and voice.
